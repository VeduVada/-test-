Rules

  • The participants must be courteous to other participants, the judges, and teachers.
  • The topics will be intimated any time before the event.
  • The participants must speak in turn. Any use of foul/derogatory language will result in immediate disqualification.
  • The judges have the right to silence any participant to allow for fair debate.
  • Participants can be asked to flip sides in the debate if required.
  • In the literary event, participants will be given topics on the spot.
  • No electronic devices will be allowed into the venue for both events. If found, action will be taken by the school.

Event outline

  • The Agree to Disagree event has 2 sub-events: namely the Literary event and the Debate event.
  • The Literary event is open for classes 1 to 8 (Category A and B).
  • The Debate event is open for classes 5 to 11 (Category B and C).
  • The students of Category A can participate in the Literary event only.
  • The students of Category B can participate either in Debate or Literary (any one).
  • The students of Category C can only participate in Debate.
  • For the Literary event, there will be 2 rounds. The themes for each round will be given on the spot and can range from poetry, writing, and recitation/storytelling.
  • The Debate event will have 3 rounds. The topic for each will be given before the round begins. Only the first round's topic will be announced before the event.
  • There will be a turn of events in each round which can make or break your chances. It is completely based on the participants' skill and knowledge to get to the top.
